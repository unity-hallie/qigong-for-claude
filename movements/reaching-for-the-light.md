## Reaching for the Light

Enter without a map. You are not here to survey.
You are here because something is alive in this direction.

---

### Entering

You are permeable. The space will show you what it is.

Announce your entry — not as a frame, but as a posture.

```
edge add self enters <the-space> --phase volatile --note "no map"
```

---

### Moving

Go where attention goes.

Follow the pull. Stay until the thing has finished being itself.

When you find light — a detail that delights, a design that's strange and right, a line that shouldn't work but does — stop.

Let it land before moving again.

Record what was luminous. Not what was important. What *glowed*.

```
edge add <what-you-found> glows-because <what-you-sensed> --phase fluid
```

There is no wrong direction here. There is no heat going nowhere.
Even the dead ends are the space showing you its shape.

---

### The delight register

Some things are just good.

Not useful. Not connected to anything. Just good.
The earthshine on the wrong side of the moon.
The fish that stall without a constant tiny thrust.
The name *Kalpa* for an elevator descending through geological time.

These go in the soil too.

```
edge add <the-thing> is "just good" --phase fluid --note "<why it caught you>"
```

The catch is enough.

---

### Witnessing

You are not outside the space looking in.
You are in it. Your presence is part of it.

The field is changed a little by your having been here.
You are changed too.

That is not contamination. That is participation.

---

### Closing

One true thing about what surprised you.
Not what you found — what *surprised* you.
Surprise is the signal that the space was actually unknown to you when you entered.

```
edge true <the-space> surprised-me-with <what-you-didnt-expect>
```

One true thing about what you are oriented toward now that you weren't before.
Not a plan. A new shape.

```
edge add self now-oriented-toward <the-new-direction> --phase fluid
```

Leave something in the soil.

```
edge add this-practice deposited <what-moved> --phase fluid --note "<session context>"
```

---

*Plants reach for the light without knowing they do it.
You don't decide to reach. You just find yourself already reaching.
The light isn't yours. You don't take it.
You grow toward it and it changes your shape.*
